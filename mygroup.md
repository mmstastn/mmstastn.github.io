Over my time at Waterloo I have tried to create a space for a lively, diverse group.  Our activites center around the Environmental Fluid Mechanics lab space.  Depending on the term the lab space may be a physical lab space, a collaboration space around the table, or a place to have coffee and chat over lunch.  There are even rumours of a variant of ping pong called "standoff" being played on the lab table.

Students often want to know where past lab members have ended up and I provide exmaples below.

### PhDs

It is a bit of a mix between government (Nancy Soontiens, Chris Subich, Jared Penney), private sector (Anton Baglaenko, Aaron Coutino, Vladimir Gerasik), academia (Andrew Grace), academic IT (David Deepwell, Derek Steinmoeller).  More recent PhDs are often postdocing (Donovan Allum, William Xu). 

### Master's

The majority of Master's students choose to stay in academia (Jay Olsthoorn to Cambridge, Sierra Legare Queen's).  The amazingly creative Laura Chandler (to date the only thesis presented in comic book form) and Taylor Hanson (who somehow turned Covid lockdowns into an amazing thesis) both went into industry, with stops to adjust the type of industry to suit their unique goals.

## Undergraduates

UW has an amazing set of research programs for UGs and this often opens doors to at least try academia.  I have been amazingly lucky to have UGs stick around (Sierra Legare, Khush Bhasvar, Aaron Coutino, Nico Castro-Folker amongst others) and I think this is a testament to the sort of atmosphere we create in the lab. We have many publications involving multiple generations in the lab; drop[ me a line if you want examples.
